FAQ

Questions, answered.

Everything you need to know before starting a model project. Still unsure? We're glad to advise on scope and scale.

General
What is an architectural scale model?

An architectural scale model is a physical representation of a building or site, built at a reduced scale to communicate design intent, massing, and spatial relationships. These models are commonly used by developers and architects for presentations, approvals, and sales environments where clarity and accuracy matter.

What types of models do you make?

We build architectural scale models, sales-gallery and presentation models, masterplan and topographic site models, product & industrial prototypes, and film and commercial miniatures. Each is customized by scale, purpose, and timeline — never a one-size-fits-all template.

Who do you work with?

Architects, real-estate developers, urban designers, film and commercial art departments, and product teams across the United States and Canada — anyone who relies on physical models as tools for alignment and action, not decoration.

Why use a physical model instead of renderings?

Drawings and renderings often require interpretation. A physical model removes interpretation by making scale, massing, circulation, and spatial relationships immediately clear to everyone in the room — so decisions happen faster and with less friction.

Pricing & getting started
How much does an architectural model cost?

Architectural scale model projects typically begin around $2,000, with final pricing based on scale, level of detail, and presentation requirements. Because each model is custom-built, pricing is confirmed after reviewing project scope and intended use.

How do I get a quote?

Share what you have — drawings, renderings, or 3D files — and tell us how the model will be used. We'll recommend the right approach and provide a clear, fixed quote before fabrication begins.

What files do you need to start?

Dimension drawings, 3D files, or reference images are typically sufficient to begin a review. We accept CAD, PDF, Revit, Rhino, and SketchUp. If some information isn't finalized, guidance can still be provided to help define the appropriate scope.

Scale & detail
What scale should my model be?

Common scales include 1:50, 1:96, and 1:144, each suited to different presentation needs. Scale selection is guided by model size, viewing distance, and the level of detail required — and recommendations are provided during review.

Can you build any scale?

Yes. We build to any scale your project requires — interiors and units through to city-scale masterplans. Actual dimensions always guide the recommendation, so the finished model is practical to view, transport, and present.

What level of detail can you achieve?

From simplified massing models for early design to highly detailed presentation models with façade treatment, glazing, materials, and integrated lighting. We focus on what a model needs to clarify — where detail adds clarity, and where simplification improves understanding.

Production & quality
How long does a model take?

Typical production timelines range from 2 to 4 weeks, depending on model complexity and revision requirements. Once project details are reviewed, a clear timeline is provided before work begins.

What materials do you use?

We combine advanced digital manufacturing — laser cutting, CNC, and 3D printing — with traditional hand craftsmanship, using durable materials suitable for transport and presentation. Components are hand-assembled with clean edges, tight joints, smooth surfaces, and accurate color.

How do you ensure quality?

Quality control is performed throughout the build, and every model goes through a detailed inspection before delivery. QC photos are shared with you along the way through the Studio Portal.

Delivery & tracking
Do you ship nationwide?

Yes. Models are packed in custom protective travel cases for secure nationwide delivery across the United States and Canada — to offices, sales galleries, and presentation venues. Shipping is reviewed as part of project planning.

Do you offer installation?

Yes. Depending on your needs, your model can arrive presentation-ready for easy self-install, or we can provide on-site delivery and setup so it's ready for presentations, exhibitions, and investor meetings.

Can I track my project's progress?

Yes. Every Carve project includes access to the Studio Portal, where you can follow milestones, approve stages, exchange files, and message the team in real time.

Still have a question?

The right first step is understanding what decision your model needs to support. Send us your project details and we'll point you in the right direction.
