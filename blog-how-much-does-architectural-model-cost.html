How Much Does an Architectural Scale Model Cost?

Architectural models are custom-built physical products that combine 3D printing, CNC machining, hand assembly, painting, and finishing. Because every project is unique, pricing is estimated once the design information is reviewed.

The ranges below are common U.S. market figures and a useful starting point for budgeting. Final pricing is confirmed after reviewing project scope and intended use.

Typical price ranges (2025, U.S.)

Concept, massing, feasibility & small residential$2,000 – $8,000
Townhomes, small commercial & moderate-detail site models$4,000 – $20,000
Sales galleries, luxury developments & high-detail presentations$15,000 – $80,000+
Urban planning, industrial, campuses & infrastructure$30,000 – $250,000+

What drives the cost

Size & scale

Larger models require more materials, more components, and more fabrication time. A shift from 1:200 to 1:100 can roughly double the cost.

Level of detail

Fine façade details, furniture, landscaping, lighting, and realistic finishes all increase material and labor hours.

Materials

Costs change based on whether the model is built from ABS, acrylic, resin, engineered wood, or a mix of premium components.

Special features

LED lighting, interchangeable modules, transparent components, moving parts, and embedded electronics add to pricing.

Timeline

Rush projects use more labor overlap and extended machine hours, which increases the overall cost.
