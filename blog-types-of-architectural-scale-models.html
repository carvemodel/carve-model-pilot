Types of Architectural Scale Models

Architectural scale models remain one of the most powerful storytelling tools in the built environment. Choosing the right type ensures clear communication and maximum impact.

Concept & massing models

Simplified block models used during early design stages to study scale, height, form, and spatial relationships. They're perfect for concept presentations, entitlement meetings, and planning reviews — where the goal is to communicate intent quickly without unnecessary detail.

Presentation models

Detailed architectural replicas highlighting façade treatment, materials, glazing, and structural expression. These are ideal for client presentations, sales centers, and public exhibitions, where finish and realism carry the message.

Masterplan & site models

Large-scale models for multi-building developments, showing massing, circulation, landscape, and site relationships. They work well for real-estate presentations, planning reviews, and public exhibitions of larger projects.

Interior models

Built at a larger scale to communicate units, hotel rooms, and interior spaces — useful when the experience of a space, not just its exterior form, is what needs to be sold or approved.
