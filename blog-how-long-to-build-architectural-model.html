How Long Does It Take to Build an Architectural Scale Model?

Typical production timelines range from 2 to 4 weeks, depending on complexity and revisions. Understanding the timeline helps developers, architects, and designers plan ahead and avoid unnecessary rush fees.

A standard production timeline

Here's how a typical build breaks down from kickoff to shipping:

File preparation, scaling & planningDay 1–3
Fabrication — 3D printing, CNC, laser cuttingDay 4–10
Painting, detailing & assemblyDay 11–18
Lighting installation & base workDay 19–22
Quality control & photo reviewDay 23–24
Packing & shippingDay 25

What affects your timeline

  • Complexity and scale — larger, multi-building models take longer to fabricate and assemble.
  • Level of detail — fine finishes, landscaping, and lighting add production time.
  • Revisions — changes after fabrication has started extend the schedule.
  • Special features — electronics, moving parts, and modular elements require additional build and test time.

Plan ahead

Deadlines compress quickly — earlier is always better. Whether you need a fast concept study or a highly detailed presentation model, early coordination produces the best results.

Preparing for an event or working to a tight schedule? We can provide a timeline estimate within hours based on your drawings and requirements.
